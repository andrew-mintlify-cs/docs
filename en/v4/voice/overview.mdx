You are inside the **Voice** product on **Version v4.0** (tag **Customers**).

Voice and **Storage** are separate products in the product switcher.

## What Voice includes

Use this product for calls, transcripts, and realtime audio sessions.

### Place a call

Start an outbound session and stream audio to your application.

#### Outbound dialing

Provide a destination number and start the session from your backend.

##### Destination formats

Use E.164 numbers for outbound destinations.

###### Example format

`+14155552671` is a valid E.164 example shape.

##### Caller ID

Choose a provisioned number your project owns.

###### Number checks

Confirm the number is enabled for outbound Voice before dialing.

#### Realtime audio stream

Connect your client to the session stream to send and receive audio.

##### Stream transport

Follow the Voice SDK transport for your platform.

###### Connection tip

Open the stream after the session reports ready.

##### Audio codecs

Prefer the codec recommended for your region and client.

###### Fallback codec

Keep a secondary codec for constrained networks.

### Capture transcripts

Store transcripts from completed sessions for search and review.

#### Automatic transcription

Enable transcription so completed calls produce text you can store.

##### Language settings

Set the expected spoken language for better accuracy.

###### Default language

Use your primary support language as the project default.

##### Retention window

Decide how long transcripts stay available.

###### Compliance note

Align retention with your privacy policy.

#### Search past sessions

Query transcripts by session ID, time range, or keyword.

##### Session ID lookup

Use the session ID when you need one exact call.

###### Fast path

ID lookup is the quickest way to open a known transcript.

##### Keyword search

Search transcript text when you do not know the session ID.

###### Search tip

Prefer distinctive phrases over very short tokens.
